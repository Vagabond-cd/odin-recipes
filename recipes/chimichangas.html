Chimichangas with Sourcream Sauce

Chickens chimichangas

I love chimichangas. And I guarantee you will too after following this recipe. For the uninformed a chimichanga is essentially a deep-fried burrito. Typically comprised of some kind of shredded or ground meat(this recipes uses chicken but you can substitute for just about anything.),cheese, onions and peppers, all wrapped up in a flour tortilla and deep-fried till golden brown. I personally enjoy them with a fresh salad of lettuce, onions, and guacamole. A healhy drizzle of sourcream sauce makes it even better.

Ingredients

-Chicken Chimichangas-

  • 1 lbs(~450g) skinless boneless chicken breasts or thighs
  • 3 cups(~700ml) of water
  • 1 Tbsp(~14g) chili powder
  • 1 tsp(~5.5g) salt
  • 1 tsp(~5.5g) ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp(~3g) ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp(~3g) garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp(~3g) onion powder
  • 1/4 tsp(1.5g) cayenne pepper
  • 1(7oz)(~200g)can of green chiles (If you want more heat substitute with fresh jalapeno)
  • 1/2 cup(~26g) diced onion
  • 3 large cloves of garlic, minced
  • oil for frying
  • 8 large flour tortillas
  • 8 oz(~225g) shredded Monterey Jack cheese

-Sour cream sauce-

  • 2 Tbsp(~28g) butter
  • 2 Tbsp(~28g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup(~236ml) water
  • 1 cube chicken bouillon
  • 1/2 cup(~118ml) sourcream
  • salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Heat a small amount of oil in a large saucepan medium-high heat. Sear chicken on both sides for 2-3 minutes.Add water, and season with chilipowder, salt, cumin, black pepper, garlic, powder, onion powder, and cayenne pepper. Bring to a simmer over high heat, then reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer for about 15 minutes.
  2. After 15 minutes, stir in 4 oz of green chiles, onion, and garlic; continue simmering until the liquid has reduced to 1 cup. Reserve remaining 3 oz of chopped chiles.
  3. Remove the chicken, shred with forks, and return to the broth
  4. Meanwhile, melt the butter in a small saucepan over medium heat. Stir in flour and cook for 1 minute. Whisk water and bouillon cube until the water has thickend and the bouillon cube has dissolved, about 4 minutes. Whisk in reserved green chiles and the sour cream; season to taste with salt and pepper. Keep warm.
  5. Heat oil in a deep-fryer or large saucepan to 375 degrees F(190 degrees C)
  6. Place a tortilla onto your work surface, then spoon about 1/3 cup of the chicken filling halfway between the bottom edge and center of the tortilla. Flatten the filling into a rectanguluar shape with the back of a spoon. Sprinkle about 1 oz of the Monterey Jack cheese over the filling.
  7. Fold the bottom of the tortilla snugly over the filling, then forld in the left and right edges. Roll the chimichanga up to the top edge, forming a tight cylinder; secure the ends with wooden toothpicks. Repeat with the remaining ingredients.
  8. Cook the chimichangas, 2 at a time in the hot oil until they are crisp and golden brown on both sides, about 1 minute per side.
  9. Drain on a paper-towel lined plate, and remove the toothpicks
  10. Top with sour cream sauce and serve.
